Celestial Bodies

A celestial body is any natural body outside of the Earth's atmosphere.

Black Hole

A black hole is a place in space where gravity is so strong that even light can not get out, this is why you cannot see a black hole normally exept with using infrared light. The gravity is so strong because matter has been squeezed into a tiny space. This can happen when a star is dying.

White Holes

A white hole is the opposite of a black hole, white holes expells objects out of it into space. A white hole is created when a black hole evaporated to a degree where it could not shrink any further because space-time could not be squeezed into anything smaller, the dying black hole would then rebound to form a white hole. There are many other theories about how a White hole can be created for example a Black hole and a white hole could be connected and the black hole sucks everything in while the white hole expells everything out. There is still a lot of unknown things about a white hole.

Galaxies

A galaxy is a huge collection of gas, dust, and billions of stars and their solar systems, all held together by gravity.

Stars

A star is a sphere of gas held together by its own gravity. At the end of a stars life the star collapses on itself and a supernova happens. Sometimes when a star is big enough when it dies it will create a blackhole
